💜 Real Patient Stories

We built this because patients told us what's broken

Every feature comes from a real frustration, a real quote, and a real need.

1

"I forgot my medication — again. And now I'm scared to double-dose."

"The alarm goes off, I snooze it, and five hours later I can't remember if I ever took my blood pressure pill. Then I get scared to double-dose. So I just… don't take it that day."

Our solution

Smart, non-shaming reminders with a "did you take it?" quick-log. Missed-dose tracking that doesn't judge, plus refill alerts when you're running low. One-tap retroactive logging for those times when pregnancy brain or brain fog wins.

💊⏰
2

"I forget to tell my doctor the most important things."

"I had a list of symptoms in my notes app. Of course I forgot to open it during the appointment. After the doctor left, I just stared at the wall. Another three months of no answers."

Our solution

One-tap "Pre-visit Summary" that pulls your symptoms, medications, and questions into a single page. Print it, email it, or show it on your phone. Never leave the exam room kicking yourself again.

📋✅
3

"I don't understand what my test results actually mean for me."

"I got an email: 'Your results are available.' I logged in, clicked the PDF, and it was full of medical codes. It said 'Mild abnormality, correlate clinically.' What does that mean? Am I dying? I panicked for a week."

Our solution

Plain-language translation layer. Every lab result, prescription instruction, and discharge note gets a clear "What This Means for You" summary — because understanding shouldn't require a medical degree.

🔬💡
4

"I need to prove my symptoms aren't just 'anxiety.'"

"I told my provider I had a headache that wouldn't go away. She wrote 'mild headache' in my chart. I ended up with severe preeclampsia. If I had shown her a printed log of my BP and symptoms, maybe she would have listened."

Our solution

The Pregnancy Companion automatically tracks blood pressure, swelling, headaches, and more — generating a trend report you can hand to your provider. Objective, date-stamped evidence that your voice matters.
